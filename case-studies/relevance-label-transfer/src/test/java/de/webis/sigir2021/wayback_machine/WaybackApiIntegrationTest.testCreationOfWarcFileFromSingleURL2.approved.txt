style="border-bottom:1px dotted">ʌ</span></a></span><span class="IPA"><a href="/web/20120111123446/http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Wikipedia:IPA_for_English#Key" title="Wikipedia:IPA for English"><span Mississippi">Harrison County, Mississippi</a>, in the <a href="/web/20120111123446/http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/United_States" title="United States">United States</a>. The 2010 <a href="/web/20120111123446/http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/United_States_Census" title="United States Census">census</a> recorded the population as 44,054. Along with <a href="/web/20120111123446/http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Gulfport,_Mississippi" title="Gulfport, Mississippi">Gulfport</a>, Biloxi is a county seat of <a href="/web/20120111123446/http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Harrison_County,_Mississippi" title="Harrison County, Mississippi">Harrison County</a>.</p>
<p>The city is part of the <a href="/web/20120111123446/http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Gulfport-Biloxi_metropolitan_area" title="Gulfport-Biloxi metropolitan area" class="mw-redirect">Gulfport-Biloxi metropolitan area</a> and the <a href="/web/20120111123446/http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Gulfport-Biloxi-Pascagoula,_Mississippi_Combined_Statistical_Area" title="Gulfport-Biloxi-Pascagoula, Mississippi Combined Statistical Area" class="mw-redirect">Gulfport-Biloxi-Pascagoula, Mississippi Combined Statistical Area</a>. Pre-<a href="/web/20120111123446/http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Hurricane_Katrina" title="Hurricane Katrina">Katrina</a>, Biloxi was the third largest city in Mississippi; but with its population losses following that storm, <a href="/web/20120111123446/http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Hattiesburg,_Mississippi" title="Hattiesburg, Mississippi">Hattiesburg</a> now has that distinction.</p>
<p>The beachfront of Biloxi lies directly on the <a href="/web/20120111123446/http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Mississippi_Sound" title="Mississippi Sound">Mississippi Sound</a>, with <a href="/web/20120111123446/http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Barrier_island" title="Barrier island">barrier islands</a> scattered off the coast and into the <a href="/web/20120111123446/http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Gulf_of_Mexico" title="Gulf of Mexico">Gulf of Mexico</a>.</p>
<p><a href="/web/20120111123446/http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Keesler_Air_Force_Base" title="Keesler Air Force Base">Keesler Air Force Base</a> lies within the city and is home to the <a href="/web/20120111123446/http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/81st_Training_Wing" title="81st Training Wing">81st Training Wing</a> and the <a href="/web/20120111123446/http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/403d_Wing" title="403d Wing">403d Wing</a> of the <a href="/web/20120111123446/http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/United_States" title="United States">U.S. Air Force</a> Reserve.</p>

<h2><span class="editsection">[<a href="/web/20120111123446/http://en.wikipedia.org/w/index.php?title=Biloxi,_Mississippi&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Geography and climate">edit</a>]</span> <span class="mw-headline" id="Geography_and_climate">Geography and climate</span></h2>
<p>Biloxi is located at <span class="plainlinks nourlexpansion"><a rel="nofollow" class="external text" href="http://web.archive.org/web/20120111123446/http://toolserver.org/~geohack/geohack.php?pagename=Biloxi,_Mississippi&amp;params=30_24_43_N_88_55_40_W_type:city"><span class="geo-default"><span class="geo-dms" title="Maps, aerial photos, and other data for this location"><span class="latitude">30°24′43″N</span> <span class="longitude">88°55′40″W</span></span></span><span class="geo-multi-punct">﻿ / ﻿</span><span class="geo-nondefault"><span class="geo-dec" title="Maps, aerial photos, and other data for this location">30.41194°N 88.92778°W</span><span style="display:none">﻿ / <span class="geo">30.41194; -88.92778</span></span></span></a></span> (30.412029, -88.927829)<sup id="cite_ref-GR1_0-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-GR1-0"><span>[</span>1<span>]</span></a></sup> and has an elevation of 20 feet (6.1&#160;m).<sup id="cite_ref-GR3_1-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-GR3-1"><span>[</span>2<span>]</span></a></sup></p>
<p>According to the <a href="/web/20120111123446/http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/United_States_Census_Bureau" title="United States Census Bureau">United States Census Bureau</a>, the city has a total area of 46.5&#160;square miles (120.5&#160;km² ). 38.0&#160;square miles (98.5&#160;km²) of it is land and 8.5&#160;square miles (22.0&#160;km²) of it is water. The total area is 18.27% water.</p>

<p>Biloxi has a subtropical climate that is heavily influenced by the Gulf of Mexico. Winter days are mild and wet. Snow is extremely rare in Biloxi. Summers are hot and humid, bearing the brunt of tropical storms during the late summer to fall. Biloxi's record low of 10°F (-12.2°C) was recorded on January 24, 1963, and the record high of 104°F (40°C) was recorded on August 29, 2000.</p>

<h2><span class="editsection">[<a href="/web/20120111123446/http://en.wikipedia.org/w/index.php?title=Biloxi,_Mississippi&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Colonial era">edit</a>]</span> <span class="mw-headline" id="Colonial_era">Colonial era</span></h2>
<p>The history of Biloxi, Mississippi, spans more than 300 years.</p>
<p>The first permanent settlement in <a href="/web/20120111123446/http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Louisiana_(New_France)" title="Louisiana (New France)">French Louisiana</a> was founded at <a href="/web/20120111123446/http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Fort_Maurepas" title="Fort Maurepas">Fort Maurepas</a>, now in <a href="/web/20120111123446/http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Ocean_Springs,_Mississippi" title="Ocean Springs, Mississippi">Ocean Springs, Mississippi</a> and referred to as Old Biloxi, in 1699 under the direction of <a href="/web/20120111123446/http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Pierre_Le_Moyne_d%27Iberville" title="Pierre Le Moyne d'Iberville">Pierre Le Moyne d'Iberville</a>, with Louisiana separated from <a href="/web/20120111123446/http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Spanish_Florida" title="Spanish Florida">Spanish Florida</a> at the <a href="/web/20120111123446/http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Perdido_River" title="Perdido River">Perdido River</a> near <a href="/web/20120111123446/http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Pensacola,_Florida" title="Pensacola, Florida">Pensacola</a> (founded 1559 and again in 1698).</p>
<p>The name of Biloxi in French was <i>"Bilocci"</i> (with <i>"fort Maurepas"</i>),<sup id="cite_ref-enquete_3-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-enquete-3"><span>[</span>4<span>]</span></a></sup> and the name was sometimes translated into English as "Fort Bilocci" on maps updated circa year 1710/1725.<sup id="cite_ref-rmaps1_4-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-rmaps1-4"><span>[</span>5<span>]</span></a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-rmaps2_5-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-rmaps2-5"><span>[</span>6<span>]</span></a></sup></p>
<p>In 1720, the administrative capital of <a href="/web/20120111123446/http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Louisiana_(New_France)" title="Louisiana (New France)">French Louisiana</a> was moved to Biloxi (or Bilocci) from <a href="/web/20120111123446/http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Mobile,_Alabama" title="Mobile, Alabama">Mobile</a> (or <i>Mobille</i>). French Louisiana (part of New France) was known in French as <i>La Louisiane</i> in colonial times, but in modern times is called <i>"La Louisiane française"</i> to distinguish from the modern state of <a href="/web/20120111123446/http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Louisiana" title="Louisiana">Louisiana</a> (also <i>"Louisiane"</i> in French).<sup id="cite_ref-enquete_3-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-enquete-3"><span>[</span>4<span>]</span></a></sup></p>
<p>Due to fears of tides and hurricanes in the 18th century, the capital of French Louisiana was later moved by colonial governor <a href="/web/20120111123446/http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Jean-Baptiste_Le_Moyne_de_Bienville" title="Jean-Baptiste Le Moyne de Bienville" class="mw-redirect">Bienville</a>, in 1723, from Biloxi to a new inland harbor town named <i>La Nouvelle-Orléans</i> (<a href="/web/20120111123446/http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/New_Orleans" title="New Orleans">New Orleans</a>), built for the purpose in 1718-1720.</p>
<p>In 1763, following Great Britain's victory in the <a href="/web/20120111123446/http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Seven_Years_War" title="Seven Years War" class="mw-redirect">Seven Years War</a>, <a href="/web/20120111123446/http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/France" title="France">France</a> had to cede French Louisiana east of the <a href="/web/20120111123446/http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Mississippi_River" title="Mississippi River">Mississippi River</a>, except for New Orleans, to <a href="/web/20120111123446/http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Great_Britain" title="Great Britain">Great Britain</a>, as part of the <a href="/web/20120111123446/http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Treaty_of_Paris_(1763)" title="Treaty of Paris (1763)">Treaty of Paris</a>. At that same time, Louisiana west of the Mississippi, including New Orleans, was ceded to <a href="/web/20120111123446/http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Spain" title="Spain">Spain</a> as part of the <a href="/web/20120111123446/http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Treaty_of_Fontainebleau_(1762)" title="Treaty of Fontainebleau (1762)">Treaty of Fontainebleau</a>.</p>
<h2><span class="editsection">[<a href="/web/20120111123446/http://en.wikipedia.org/w/index.php?title=Biloxi,_Mississippi&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Subsequent history">edit</a>]</span> <span class="mw-headline" id="Subsequent_history">Subsequent history</span></h2>
<div class="thumb tleft">
<div class="thumbinner" style="width:252px;"><a href="/web/20120111123446/http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/File:Howard_Street_Biloxi_Mississippi_1906.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//web.archive.org/web/20120111123446im_/http://upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/5/5c/Howard_Street_Biloxi_Mississippi_1906.jpg/250px-Howard_Street_Biloxi_Mississippi_1906.jpg" width="250" height="121" class="thumbimage"/></a>
<div class="thumbcaption">
<div class="magnify"><a href="/web/20120111123446/http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/File:Howard_Street_Biloxi_Mississippi_1906.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"><img src="//web.archive.org/web/20120111123446im_/http://bits.wikimedia.org/skins-1.18/common/images/magnify-clip.png" width="15" height="11" alt=""/></a></div>
Looking West down Howard Avenue at Lameuse Street in Biloxi in 1906</div>
</div>
</div>
<p>British rule persisted from 1763 to 1779, and then Spanish rule from 1779 to 1798. Despite this, the character of Biloxi remained mostly French.<sup id="cite_ref-hco_6-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-hco-6"><span>[</span>7<span>]</span></a></sup> In 1811, Biloxi came under United States of America control as part of the <a href="/web/20120111123446/http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Mississippi_Territory" title="Mississippi Territory">Mississippi Territory</a>. Mississippi, and Biloxi with it, were then admitted to the union in 1817.</p>
<p>Now that ownership was settled, Biloxi began to grow. It became a summer resort, with the advantages of close proximity to New Orleans and ease of access via water. Summer homes were built by well-to-do farmers and commercial figures. Hotels and rental cottages came into existence to serve those who could not afford their own homes.<sup id="cite_ref-hco_6-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-hco-6"><span>[</span>7<span>]</span></a></sup></p>
<div class="thumb tright">
<div class="thumbinner" style="width:252px;"><a href="/web/20120111123446/http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/File:Biloxi_Lighthouse_2010.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//web.archive.org/web/20120111123446im_/http://upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/3/3e/Biloxi_Lighthouse_2010.jpg/250px-Biloxi_Lighthouse_2010.jpg" width="250" height="333" class="thumbimage"/></a>
<div class="thumbcaption">
<div class="magnify"><a href="/web/20120111123446/http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/File:Biloxi_Lighthouse_2010.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"><img src="//web.archive.org/web/20120111123446im_/http://bits.wikimedia.org/skins-1.18/common/images/magnify-clip.png" width="15" height="11" alt=""/></a></div>
<a href="/web/20120111123446/http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Biloxi_Lighthouse" title="Biloxi Lighthouse" class="mw-redirect">Biloxi Lighthouse</a>, built in 1848 and reputed to be one of the most photographed objects in the American South.<sup id="cite_ref-blh_7-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-blh-7"><span>[</span>8<span>]</span></a></sup></div>
</div>
</div>
<p>One of Biloxi's most known features has been the <a href="/web/20120111123446/http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Biloxi_Lighthouse" title="Biloxi Lighthouse" class="mw-redirect">Biloxi Lighthouse</a>, which was built in <a href="/web/20120111123446/http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Baltimore" title="Baltimore">Baltimore</a> and then shipped south and completed in May 1848.<sup id="cite_ref-blh_7-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-blh-7"><span>[</span>8<span>]</span></a></sup> (It and another are the only surviving lighthouses of twelve that once dotted the <a href="/web/20120111123446/http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Mississippi_Gulf_Coast" title="Mississippi Gulf Coast">Mississippi Gulf Coast</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-blh_7-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-blh-7"><span>[</span>8<span>]</span></a></sup>)</p>
<p>In the early stages of the <a href="/web/20120111123446/http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/American_Civil_War" title="American Civil War">Civil War</a>, <a href="/web/20120111123446/http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Ship_Island_(Mississippi)" title="Ship Island (Mississippi)">Ship Island</a> was captured by <a href="/web/20120111123446/http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Union_Army" title="Union Army">Union forces</a>, which led to the effective Union capture of Biloxi as well. No major battles were fought in the area, and Biloxi did not suffer direct damage from the war.<sup id="cite_ref-hco_6-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-hco-6"><span>[</span>7<span>]</span></a></sup> Some local Union sentiment could be discerned following the war's conclusion.<sup id="cite_ref-blh_7-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-blh-7"><span>[</span>8<span>]</span></a></sup></p>
<p>In the postbellum period, Biloxi again emerged as a vacation spot. Its popularity as a destination increased with railroad access. In 1881, the first <a href="/web/20120111123446/http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Cannery" title="Cannery" class="mw-redirect">cannery</a> was built in the town, leading to others soon joining the location. Biloxi grew again, and as different <a href="/web/20120111123446/http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Ethnic_group" title="Ethnic group">ethnic groups</a> came to work in the seafood factories, Biloxi gained a more heterogeneous population.<sup id="cite_ref-hco_6-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-hco-6"><span>[</span>7<span>]</span></a></sup></p>
<p>During <a href="/web/20120111123446/http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/World_War_II" title="World War II">World War II</a>, the <a href="/web/20120111123446/http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/United_States_Army_Air_Forces" title="United States Army Air Forces">United States Army Air Forces</a> built Keesler Field, now <a href="/web/20120111123446/http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Keesler_Air_Force_Base" title="Keesler Air Force Base">Keesler Air Force Base</a>, which became a major <a href="/web/20120111123446/http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Basic_training" title="Basic training" class="mw-redirect">basic training</a> site and site for aircraft maintenance. The Biloxi economy boomed as a result,<sup id="cite_ref-isjl_8-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-isjl-8"><span>[</span>9<span>]</span></a></sup> again bringing more diverse groups to the area. By 1958, the first <a href="/web/20120111123446/http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Synagogue" title="Synagogue">Jewish synagogue</a> had been built in the town.<sup id="cite_ref-isjl_8-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-isjl-8"><span>[</span>9<span>]</span></a></sup></p>
<p>Biloxi's <a href="/web/20120111123446/http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Casino" title="Casino">casino</a> history dates back to a period in the 1940s, when open, if technically illegal, gambling took place in a casino within the <a href="/web/20120111123446/http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Broadwater_Beach_Resort" title="Broadwater Beach Resort" class="mw-redirect">Broadwater Beach Resort</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-sh-landmark_9-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-sh-landmark-9"><span>[</span>10<span>]</span></a></sup> Open gambling ended during the 1950s.<sup id="cite_ref-sh-before_10-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-sh-before-10"><span>[</span>11<span>]</span></a></sup> The <a href="/web/20120111123446/http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Mississippi_Gulf_Coast" title="Mississippi Gulf Coast">Mississippi Gulf Coast</a> became known as the "Poor Man's Riviera", and was frequented by <a href="/web/20120111123446/http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Southern_United_States" title="Southern United States">Southern</a> families interested in fishing expeditions during the summer.<sup id="cite_ref-nyt-1963_11-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-nyt-1963-11"><span>[</span>12<span>]</span></a></sup> Commercially, Biloxi was dominated by shrimp boats and oyster luggers.<sup id="cite_ref-nyt-1963_11-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-nyt-1963-11"><span>[</span>12<span>]</span></a></sup></p>
<p>In the early 1960s, the Gulf Coast again emerged as a prime alternative to <a href="/web/20120111123446/http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Florida" title="Florida">Florida</a> as a southern vacation destination among <a href="/web/20120111123446/http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Northern_United_States" title="Northern United States">Northerners</a>, with Biloxi a center of the focus.<sup id="cite_ref-nyt-1963_11-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-nyt-1963-11"><span>[</span>12<span>]</span></a></sup> Biloxi hotels upgraded their amenities and hired chefs from France and Switzerland in an effort to provide some of the best seafood cuisine in the country.<sup id="cite_ref-nyt-1963_11-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-nyt-1963-11"><span>[</span>12<span>]</span></a></sup></p>
<p>With the introduction of legal gambling in Mississippi in the 1990s, Biloxi was again transformed.<sup id="cite_ref-isjl_8-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-isjl-8"><span>[</span>9<span>]</span></a></sup> It became an important center for casinos, and the hotels and complexes brought millions of dollars in tourism revenue to the city. The more famous casino complexes were the <a href="/web/20120111123446/http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Beau_Rivage_(Mississippi)" title="Beau Rivage (Mississippi)">Beau Rivage</a> casino resort, the <a href="/web/20120111123446/http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Hard_Rock_Hotel_and_Casino_(Biloxi)" title="Hard Rock Hotel and Casino (Biloxi)">Hard Rock Hotel and Casino (Biloxi)</a>, <a href="/web/20120111123446/http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Casino_Magic_Biloxi_Casino_%26_Hotel" title="Casino Magic Biloxi Casino &amp; Hotel">Casino Magic</a>, <a href="/web/20120111123446/http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Grand_Casino_Biloxi" title="Grand Casino Biloxi">Grand Casino</a>, <a href="/web/20120111123446/http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Isle_of_Capri_Casinos" title="Isle of Capri Casinos">Isle of Capri Casino Resort Biloxi</a>, <a href="/web/20120111123446/http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Penn_National_Gaming" title="Penn National Gaming">Boomtown Casino</a>, <a href="/web/20120111123446/http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/President_Casino_Broadwater_Resort" title="President Casino Broadwater Resort">President Casino Broadwater Resort</a>, and <a href="/web/20120111123446/http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/IP_Hotel_and_Casino" title="IP Hotel and Casino" class="mw-redirect">Imperial Palace</a>. Like <a href="/web/20120111123446/http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Tunica_County,_Mississippi" title="Tunica County, Mississippi">Tunica County</a> in the northern part of the state, Biloxi and the surrounding Gulf Coast region was considered a leading gambling center in the <a href="/web/20120111123446/http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Southern_United_States" title="Southern United States">Southern United States</a>.</p>
<p>To celebrate the area's Tricentennial in 1998/99, the city's tourism promotion agency invited the nationally-syndicated Travel World Radio Show to broadcast live from Biloxi, with co-host Willem Bagchus in attendance.</p>
<p>By the early 21st century, Biloxi's economy rested on the three prongs of seafood, tourism and gaming.<sup id="cite_ref-hco_6-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-hco-6"><span>[</span>7<span>]</span></a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="editsection">[<a href="/web/20120111123446/http://en.wikipedia.org/w/index.php?title=Biloxi,_Mississippi&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Hurricanes">edit</a>]</span> <span class="mw-headline" id="Hurricanes">Hurricanes</span></h2>
<p>Scores of hurricanes have hit the Mississippi Gulf Coast, but the most destructive, as measured by storm surge levels in the <a href="/web/20120111123446/http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Biloxi_Light" title="Biloxi Light">Biloxi Lighthouse</a>, occurred in 1855, 1906, 1909, 1947, 1969 (<a href="/web/20120111123446/http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Hurricane_Camille" title="Hurricane Camille">Hurricane Camille</a>), and 2005 (<a href="/web/20120111123446/http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Hurricane_Katrina" title="Hurricane Katrina">Hurricane Katrina</a>)<sup id="cite_ref-12" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-12"><span>[</span>13<span>]</span></a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="editsection">[<a href="/web/20120111123446/http://en.wikipedia.org/w/index.php?title=Biloxi,_Mississippi&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Hurricane Katrina">edit</a>]</span> <span class="mw-headline" id="Hurricane_Katrina">Hurricane Katrina</span></h3>

<p>On August 29, 2005, <a href="/web/20120111123446/http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Hurricane_Katrina" title="Hurricane Katrina">Hurricane Katrina</a> hit the <a href="/web/20120111123446/http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Mississippi_Gulf_Coast" title="Mississippi Gulf Coast">Mississippi Gulf Coast</a> with high winds, heavy rains and a 27-foot (8.2&#160;m) storm surge, causing massive damage to the area. Katrina came ashore during the high tide of 6:56AM, +2.3&#160;feet more.<sup id="cite_ref-13" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-13"><span>[</span>14<span>]</span></a></sup> Commenting on the&#160;power of the storm and the damage, Mayor <a href="/web/20120111123446/http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/A.J._Holloway" title="A.J. Holloway" class="mw-redirect">A.J. Holloway</a> said, "This is our <a href="/web/20120111123446/http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/2004_Indian_Ocean_earthquake" title="2004 Indian Ocean earthquake" class="mw-redirect">tsunami</a>."<sup id="cite_ref-14" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-14"><span>[</span>15<span>]</span></a></sup> Mississippi Governor <a href="/web/20120111123446/http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Haley_Barbour" title="Haley Barbour">Haley Barbour</a> was quoted as saying the destruction of the Mississippi coastline by <a href="/web/20120111123446/http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Hurricane_Katrina" title="Hurricane Katrina">Hurricane Katrina</a> looked like an American <a href="/web/20120111123446/http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Hiroshima" title="Hiroshima">Hiroshima</a>.</p>
<p>On the morning of August 31, 2005, in an interview on <a href="/web/20120111123446/http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/MSNBC" title="MSNBC">MSNBC</a>, Governor Barbour stated that 90% of the buildings along the coast in Biloxi and neighboring <a href="/web/20120111123446/http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Gulfport,_Mississippi" title="Gulfport, Mississippi">Gulfport</a> had been destroyed by the hurricane. Several of the "floating" casinos were torn off their supports and thrown inland, contributing to the damage.</p>
<p>Many churches were destroyed or severely damaged, including St. Michael's Catholic Church (see photo at right), which was gutted by the storm surge, breaking the entry doors and stained-glass windows along the first floor; however, the interior was later removed, and the structure was still solid enough to allow repairing the church.</p>
<p>Hurricane Katrina damaged over 40 Mississippi libraries, flooding several feet in the Biloxi Public Library and breaking windows, beyond repair, requiring a total rebuild.<sup id="cite_ref-15" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-15"><span>[</span>16<span>]</span></a></sup></p>
<p>Hurricane-force winds persisted for 17 hours and tore the branches off many coastal oak trees, but the tree trunks survived the 30-foot (9.1&#160;m) flood and many have since regrown smaller branches. Some reconstructed homes still have the <a href="/web/20120111123446/http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Antebellum_architecture" title="Antebellum architecture">antebellum</a> appearance, and miles inland, with less flooding, shopping centers have re-opened.</p>
<p>Harrison County <a href="/web/20120111123446/http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Coroner" title="Coroner">Coroner</a> Gary T. Hargrove told the mayor and City Council that Hurricane Katrina had claimed 53 victims in Biloxi, as of January 30, 2006. Of the 53 confirmed fatalities in Biloxi, a figure that includes one unidentified male, Hargrove said the average age was 58, with the youngest being 22 and the oldest 90; 14 were female and 39 were male.</p>
<p>Biloxi is also the site of a well-known memorial to the Katrina victims, built by the crew and volunteers of <i><a href="/web/20120111123446/http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Extreme_Makeover:_Home_Edition" title="Extreme Makeover: Home Edition">Extreme Makeover: Home Edition</a></i>.<sup id="cite_ref-16" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-16"><span>[</span>17<span>]</span></a></sup></p>
<p>Many casinos were damaged or destroyed by Hurricane Katrina. Of the casinos that were located in Biloxi, eight have reopened since Katrina. They are: the Grand Biloxi Casino Hotel Spa(formerly known as Grand Casino Biloxi), the Hard Rock Hotel &amp; Casino, the Isle of Capri Casino and Resort, the Palace Casino Resort, the IP Casino Resort Spa (formerly known as Imperial Palace), Treasure Bay Casino, Boomtown Casino, and the Beau Rivage, which reopened on the first anniversary of Hurricane Katrina.<sup id="cite_ref-BixCasinos_17-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-BixCasinos-17"><span>[</span>18<span>]</span></a></sup></p>
<p>Multiple plans have been laid out to rebuild the waterfront areas of Biloxi, and the federal government has recently announced that it is considering giving up to 17,000 Mississippi coast homeowners the option to sell their properties so that a vast hurricane-protection zone can be implemented.<sup id="cite_ref-18" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-18"><span>[</span>19<span>]</span></a></sup> Meanwhile, the city of Biloxi is rapidly implementing plans to allow the redevelopment of commercial properties south of highway 90.<sup id="cite_ref-19" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-19"><span>[</span>20<span>]</span></a></sup></p>

<p>Biloxi is the smaller of two principal cities of the <a href="/web/20120111123446/http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Gulfport,_Mississippi" title="Gulfport, Mississippi">Gulfport</a>-Biloxi, Mississippi <a href="/web/20120111123446/http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Metropolitan_Statistical_Area" title="Metropolitan Statistical Area">Metropolitan Statistical Area</a>, which is included in the Gulport-Biloxi-Pascagoula <a href="/web/20120111123446/http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Combined_Statistical_Area" title="Combined Statistical Area" class="mw-redirect">Combined Statistical Area</a>.</p>
<p>As of the <a href="/web/20120111123446/http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Census" title="Census">census</a><sup id="cite_ref-GR2_20-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-GR2-20"><span>[</span>21<span>]</span></a></sup> of 2000, there were 50,644&#160;people, 19,588 households, and 12,379 families residing in the city. The <a href="/web/20120111123446/http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Population_density" title="Population density">population density</a> was 1,331.8 per square mile (514.2/km²). There were 22,115 housing units at an average density of 581.6 per square mile (224.5/km²). The racial makeup of the city was 71.43% <a href="/web/20120111123446/http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/White_(U.S._Census)" title="White (U.S. Census)" class="mw-redirect">European American</a>, 19.04% <a href="/web/20120111123446/http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/African_American_(U.S._Census)" title="African American (U.S. Census)" class="mw-redirect">African American</a>, 0.49% <a href="/web/20120111123446/http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Native_American_(U.S._Census)" title="Native American (U.S. Census)" class="mw-redirect">Native American</a>, 5.11% <a href="/web/20120111123446/http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Asian_(U.S._Census)" title="Asian (U.S. Census)" class="mw-redirect">Asian</a>, 0.11% <a href="/web/20120111123446/http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Pacific_Islander_(U.S._Census)" title="Pacific Islander (U.S. Census)" class="mw-redirect">Pacific Islander</a>, 1.43% from <a href="/web/20120111123446/http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Race_(U.S._Census)" title="Race (U.S. Census)" class="mw-redirect">other races</a>, and 2.38% from two or more races. 3.65% of the population are <a href="/web/20120111123446/http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Hispanic_(U.S._Census)" title="Hispanic (U.S. Census)" class="mw-redirect">Hispanic</a> or <a href="/web/20120111123446/http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Latino_(U.S._Census)" title="Latino (U.S. Census)" class="mw-redirect">Latino</a> of any race.</p>
<p>There were 19,588 households, out of which 31.4% have children under the age of 18 living with them, 44.6% were <a href="/web/20120111123446/http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Marriage" title="Marriage">married couples</a> living together, 14.0% had a female householder with no husband present, and 36.8% are non-families. 30.1% of all households were made up of individuals and 10.6% had someone living alone who is 65 years of age or older. The average household size was 2.42 and the average family size is 3.02.</p>
<p>In the city the population dispersal was 24.2% under the age of 18, 14.3% from 18 to 24, 30.3% from 25 to 44, 19.2% from 45 to 64, and 12.0% who were 65 years of age or older. The median age was 32 years. For every 100 females there were 101.9 males. For every 100 females age 18 and over, there were 101.6 males. The median income for a household in the city was $34,106, and the median income for a family was $40,685. Males had a median income of $28,046 versus $21,267 for females. The <a href="/web/20120111123446/http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Per_capita_income" title="Per capita income">per capita income</a> for the city was $17,809. 14.6% of the population and 11.2% of families lived below the <a href="/web/20120111123446/http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Poverty_line" title="Poverty line" class="mw-redirect">poverty line</a>. Out of the total population, 19.6% of those under the age of 18 and 11.7% of those 65 and older were living below the poverty line.</p>
<p>In 2005, as a result of <a href="/web/20120111123446/http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Hurricane_Katrina" title="Hurricane Katrina">Hurricane Katrina</a>, many <a href="/web/20120111123446/http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Vietnamese_American" title="Vietnamese American">Vietnamese Americans</a> left Biloxi. In 2009 members of the Vietnamese community in Biloxi said that Vietnamese are coming back to Biloxi due to a poor economic scenario in other parts of the United States.<sup id="cite_ref-21" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-21"><span>[</span>22<span>]</span></a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="editsection">[<a href="/web/20120111123446/http://en.wikipedia.org/w/index.php?title=Biloxi,_Mississippi&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: Education">edit</a>]</span> <span class="mw-headline" id="Education">Education</span></h2>
<p>The City of Biloxi is served by the <a href="/web/20120111123446/http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Biloxi_Public_School_District" title="Biloxi Public School District">Biloxi Public School District</a> and the <a href="/web/20120111123446/http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Harrison_County_School_District" title="Harrison County School District">Harrison County School District</a>.</p>
<p>The Gulf Coast has a large Catholic school system, with which Biloxi hosts 15 of their schools.<sup id="cite_ref-22" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-22"><span>[</span>23<span>]</span></a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="editsection">[<a href="/web/20120111123446/http://en.wikipedia.org/w/index.php?title=Biloxi,_Mississippi&amp;action=edit&amp;section=8" title="Edit section: Government and infrastructure">edit</a>]</span> <span class="mw-headline" id="Government_and_infrastructure">Government and infrastructure</span></h2>
<p>The Bolton State Office Building in Biloxi includes the headquarters of the <a href="/web/20120111123446/http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Mississippi_Department_of_Marine_Resources" title="Mississippi Department of Marine Resources">Mississippi Department of Marine Resources</a> and the South Regional Office of the <a href="/web/20120111123446/http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Mississippi_Department_of_Environmental_Quality" title="Mississippi Department of Environmental Quality">Mississippi Department of Environmental Quality</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-23" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-23"><span>[</span>24<span>]</span></a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-24" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-24"><span>[</span>25<span>]</span></a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-25" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-25"><span>[</span>26<span>]</span></a></sup></p>
<p>The <a href="/web/20120111123446/http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/United_States_Postal_Service" title="United States Postal Service">United States Postal Service</a> operates the Biloxi Post Office and other area post offices.<sup id="cite_ref-26" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-26"><span>[</span>27<span>]</span></a></sup></p>

Shapiro, author of the article "Biloxi Confidential" on the <a href="/web/20120111123446/http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Crime_Library" title="Crime Library">Crime Library</a>, said that Biloxi, "like most mid-sized Southern cities," "was essentially a small town, and its ethnic urban neighborhoods were even smaller and more tightly knit."<sup id="cite_ref-27" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-27"><span>[</span>28<span>]</span></a></sup></p>

<h2><span class="editsection">[<a href="/web/20120111123446/http://en.wikipedia.org/w/index.php?title=Biloxi,_Mississippi&amp;action=edit&amp;section=11" title="Edit section: Transportation">edit</a>]</span> <span class="mw-headline" id="Transportation">Transportation</span></h2>
<p>Biloxi is served by the <a href="/web/20120111123446/http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Gulfport-Biloxi_International_Airport" title="Gulfport-Biloxi International Airport">Gulfport-Biloxi International Airport</a> in <a href="/web/20120111123446/http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Gulfport,_Mississippi" title="Gulfport, Mississippi">Gulfport, Mississippi</a>.</p>
<p>Biloxi's main highway is <a href="/web/20120111123446/http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/U.S._Highway_90" title="U.S. Highway 90" class="mw-redirect">U.S. Highway 90</a> (Beach Boulevard), which runs along the beach and by the casinos. It connects the city to Gulfport and points westward and to <a href="/web/20120111123446/http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Ocean_Springs,_Mississippi" title="Ocean Springs, Mississippi">Ocean Springs</a> and <a href="/web/20120111123446/http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Pascagoula,_Mississippi" title="Pascagoula, Mississippi">Pascagoula</a> to the east. The Biloxi Bay Bridge, connecting Biloxi and Ocean Springs, was rebuilt after Hurricane Katrina, and was fully reopened in April 2008.</p>
<p><a href="/web/20120111123446/http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Interstate_10" title="Interstate 10">Interstate 10</a> passes through the northern sections of the city, connecting the city to <a href="/web/20120111123446/http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/New_Orleans" title="New Orleans">New Orleans</a>, <a href="/web/20120111123446/http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Houston" title="Houston">Houston</a>, <a href="/web/20120111123446/http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Mobile,_Alabama" title="Mobile, Alabama">Mobile</a> and <a href="/web/20120111123446/http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Jacksonville,_Florida" title="Jacksonville, Florida">Jacksonville</a>. <a href="/web/20120111123446/http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Interstate_110_(Mississippi)" title="Interstate 110 (Mississippi)">Interstate 110</a> splits off from I-10 at <a href="/web/20120111123446/http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/D%27Iberville,_Mississippi" title="D'Iberville, Mississippi">D'Iberville</a> and heads south across the Back Bay of Biloxi to U.S. 90 near Beau Rivage, providing the city with an important hurricane evacuation route.</p>
<p>Other highways serving the area include:</p>
<ul>
<li><a href="/web/20120111123446/http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Mississippi_Highway_15" title="Mississippi Highway 15">Mississippi Highway 15</a> (Runs concurrent with I-110 for the first few miles)</li>
<li><a href="/web/20120111123446/http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Mississippi_Highway_67" title="Mississippi Highway 67">Mississippi Highway 67</a></li>
</ul>
<h2><span class="editsection">[<a href="/web/20120111123446/http://en.wikipedia.org/w/index.php?title=Biloxi,_Mississippi&amp;action=edit&amp;section=12" title="Edit section: Sports">edit</a>]</span> <span class="mw-headline" id="Sports">Sports</span></h2>
<p>In the center of what fisheries biologists term "The Fertile Fisheries Crescent", Biloxi offers some of the finest sportsfishing along the entire northern coast of the Gulf of Mexico. <a href="/web/20120111123446/http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Spotted_seatrout" title="Spotted seatrout" class="mw-redirect">Spotted seatrout</a>, <a href="/web/20120111123446/http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Red_drum" title="Red drum">red drum</a>, <a href="/web/20120111123446/http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Atlantic_Spanish_mackerel" title="Atlantic Spanish mackerel">Spanish</a> and <a href="/web/20120111123446/http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/King_mackerel" title="King mackerel">king mackerel</a>, <a href="/web/20120111123446/http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Flounder" title="Flounder">flounder</a>, <a href="/web/20120111123446/http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Lutjanidae" title="Lutjanidae">snapper</a>, <a href="/web/20120111123446/http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Grouper" title="Grouper">grouper</a>, <a href="/web/20120111123446/http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Shark" title="Shark">sharks</a>, and more are all available to anglers during the fishing season. It is not known how Hurricane Katrina affected this <a href="/web/20120111123446/http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Ecosystem" title="Ecosystem">ecosystem</a>.</p>
<p>The city is home to the <a href="/web/20120111123446/http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Mississippi_Surge" title="Mississippi Surge">Mississippi Surge</a>, a minor league hockey team playing in the <a href="/web/20120111123446/http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Southern_Professional_Hockey_League" title="Southern Professional Hockey League">Southern Professional Hockey League</a>. Home games are played at the <a href="/web/20120111123446/http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Mississippi_Coast_Coliseum" title="Mississippi Coast Coliseum">Mississippi Coast Coliseum</a>.</p>
